About me

Engineer. Educator.
Community Builder.

I am a Robotics & AI Engineer with hands-on experience designing and deploying multi-sensor perception systems combining LiDAR, depth cameras, and IMU for real-world autonomy and 3D mapping.

At GlobalDWS, I build end-to-end AI pipelines on NVIDIA Jetson hardware, contribute to digital twin workflows, and integrate cloud-connected telemetry via Azure IoT Central. My work spans from raw point-cloud capture to spatial intelligence at scale.

I hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science from the American University of Beirut (USAID Full Scholarship, Dean's Honor List) and completed an exchange semester at Carnegie Mellon University – Qatar focusing on Machine Learning and AI Ethics.

Beyond engineering, I teach STEM and digital literacy to youth and women through NGOs in North Lebanon — because technology is only powerful when it's accessible.

My work focuses on analyzing and optimizing ML pipelines under real-world constraints, including latency, accuracy, and resource efficiency on embedded hardware. I am particularly interested in bridging the gap between machine learning models and system-level performance.
